- `--source_lp_quality 0.2` (this is the `gamma` shown in the run name)
- Dataset-level source-mask stats are identical in both runs (from `metrics/label_source_stats.json`):
  - `frac_gt = 0.0911`, `frac_lp = 0.9089`
  - `r = N_gt/N_lp = 0.100218`
  - In “decoupled” mode, this implies `w_lp = r * gamma ≈ 0.020044` **if using dataset-scope `r`**.

## The difference between the two runs

The only behavioral difference is:
- `--source_imbalance_scope dataset` vs `--source_imbalance_scope batch`

This is also the only meaningful diff in `args.json` (besides `output_dir`).

### What that flag means in practice

**`dataset` scope**
- Compute a **fixed** `r = N_gt/N_lp` once from all training source masks.
- Use a single `w_lp = r * gamma` for the whole run.
- In `train.log`, this is reported as:
  - `dataset ratio r = N_gt/N_lp: 0.100218`
  - `effective w_lp = w_gt * r * gamma: 0.020044`
  - `effective mass ratio q ...: 0.200000`

**`batch` scope**
- Compute `r_batch` **per cropped batch** (so it varies with sampling/cropping).
- The log shows `r_batch` mean is ~0.048 for most epochs, which is ~2× smaller than the dataset-wide `r=0.1002`.
  - This means the effective per-batch `w_lp ≈ r_batch * gamma ≈ 0.0096` most of the time (so pseudo-label contribution is reduced vs `dataset` scope).

## Extra notes / hypotheses (why better pseudo labels may not improve trained model Dice)

Not proven here, but a few plausible explanations consistent with the saved artifacts:

1) **The improved pseudo labels may be under-weighted.**
   - With `gamma=0.2`, pseudo-label voxels already get a small weight.
   - With `--source_imbalance_scope batch`, the observed `r_batch` mean (~0.048) further reduces pseudo-label contribution vs dataset-scope runs.

2) **The “+0.07 Dice” is measured on train GT overlap, not necessarily the error modes that matter for learning.**
   - Intensity-aware affinity could reduce average error but still introduce “hard” structured mistakes that are more harmful during training (e.g., confidently wrong regions).

3) **Not apples-to-apples vs the higher-performing coords-only training runs you remember.**
   - Many stronger Graph-LP training runs in `runs/` use *outer-bg / adaptive* variants and a different tuned `gamma` (e.g. `train_graph_lp_k10_a0.9_new_n12000_outerbg_adaptive_...`), while these two quantiles16 runs use labels from `graph_lp_3desc_eval/...` (no `outerbg` in the label-generation path).
